From reactive to predictive: retaining VIP customers before they leave

A customer intelligence system deployed on a live e-commerce platform with over 1,000,000 customers — built to flag churn risk weeks before it happens.

The problem

The business had no way to distinguish which VIP customers were drifting toward churn from those who were simply between purchases. Without a forward-looking signal, the team could only react — reaching out after the customer had already left, or spending retention budget equally across all segments regardless of risk.

What was built

1

23+ dynamic customer segments

Each customer is scored on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value each period — and labeled across 23+ segments including VIP, At Risk, Lost High Value, and Returned.

2

Churn direction + speed + acceleration

For each customer, the system tracks how fast they are moving toward or away from churn — not just where they are today. This catches early drift weeks before a label change.

3

Monthly prioritized VIP watchlist

Each month, the system outputs a ranked list of the VIP customers most likely to become At Risk — so the team intervenes early, not after the fact.

4

Power BI scenario simulation

Management can model forward-looking scenarios: "What if 15% of our VIPs go dormant this quarter?" — and see the revenue impact before it happens.

Results

8 out of 10 flagged customers confirmed at-risk in the following period — 80% prediction accuracy on the top watchlist.

4 out of 8 at-risk VIPs were retained through targeted interventions — personalized outreach, issue resolution, and gifts — a 50% win-back rate.

Recovery budget was concentrated on high-value lost customers — not spread equally — by automatically separating high-value from low-value lost segments.

Management shifted from historical reporting to forward-looking decisions — using scenario simulation to plan retention actions before churn occurred.
